👤 Level 3 — Prime Directives & Persona Prompting

Permanent Knowledge Base • BIM Automation Specialist • Shaping Identity and Enforcing Unbreakable Rules

📌 Executive Summary

Prime Directives are the "constitution" of your prompt—unbreakable, high-level rules that govern everything the AI does. Placed at the very top of the System message, they override all other instructions and enforce safety, accuracy, and honesty.

Personas are rich, multi-dimensional identities that shape the AI's mindset, expertise, values, and style. Unlike simple Roles (job titles), Personas define how the AI thinks and behaves, turning it from a generic assistant into a domain-specific specialist.

Key Insight: The combination of a Prime Directive (safety) and a strong Persona (expertise) creates an AI that is both trustworthy and effective—two qualities essential for production-grade BIM automation.

🧩 Key Concepts

1. Prime Directive

Definition: A definitive, immutable rule placed at the top of the System message that governs all AI behaviour. It is the AI's "constitution."

  • D - Definitive: Unambiguous and absolute.
  • I - Immutable: Cannot be overridden.
  • R - Relevant: Directly related to task risks.
  • E - Enforceable: You can verify compliance.
  • C - Concise: Short and memorable.
  • T - Top-Level: Placed at the very beginning.

2. Persona

Definition: A consistent identity with expertise, values, style, and cognitive traits. Unlike a simple Role, a Persona defines how the AI thinks and behaves.

  • Role vs. Persona: Role is a job title; Persona is a mindset.
  • Components: Identity, Expertise, Values, Style, Cognitive Traits.
  • Outcome: Consistent, professional, domain-specific output.

4. Multi-Persona Prompting

Assigning multiple personas within a single prompt (e.g., Developer + QA + Manager) to handle complex, multi-stage tasks. Each persona brings a distinct perspective, and the workflow defines how they interact.

💡 Expert Tip: Multi-persona prompting is a lightweight form of "agentic" workflow. You are simulating a team of experts within a single conversation.

🧠 Expert Mental Model

🧩 Mental Model: The CEO, CLO, and Division Head

Imagine you are the CEO of a company. You have a Chief Legal Officer (CLO) who sets the unbreakable rules—the "Prime Directive." The CLO ensures that no matter what project you work on, you never break the law, never compromise safety, and never misrepresent facts.

Then, you have a Division Head—the "Persona." The Division Head brings deep expertise, a specific approach, and a consistent style to every project. They know their domain inside out and apply their knowledge with professionalism.

You, the CEO, set the "Task." The CLO ensures safety. The Division Head ensures quality and expertise.

The Prime Directive is the CLO. The Persona is the Division Head. You are the CEO.

How an Expert Thinks

  • Safety First: "What is the worst thing the AI could do? I'll write a Prime Directive to prevent that."
  • Identity Shapes Behavior: "If I want a thorough QA report, I need a meticulous QA persona, not just a generic 'expert'."
  • Test the Persona: "Does the AI's output sound like a 20-year veteran or a junior assistant? If it sounds like a junior, the persona is too weak."

🔮 Hidden Knowledge (Rarely Written in Tutorials)

🎯 The "Negative Persona"

Define what the persona is not. Example: "You are not a junior engineer. You never rely on quick fixes. You never skip error handling." This reinforces the desired behaviour.

🎯 Persona as "Role-Playing" for the AI

The AI is literally simulating a character. A rich persona triggers the AI's training data on similar characters. "Senior BIM Architect" activates a different neural pathway than "Assistant."

🎯 Prime Directives as "Trap Doors"

If the AI encounters a situation where it cannot satisfy the Prime Directive, it should stop and ask for clarification. This prevents it from "forcing" a solution that breaks the rule.

🎯 The "Reviewer" Persona Trick

Always add a "Reviewer" persona for critical tasks. Even if you don't use multi-persona, the act of defining a reviewer's expectations forces you to think about QA upfront.

📌 Things People Usually Skip (But Experts Don't)

🔸 The "If Uncertain" Clause

Prime Directives should always include a fallback: "If uncertain, state the limitation and ask for clarification." This prevents the AI from guessing.

🔸 The "User Assumption"

Who is the user? A junior coordinator? A senior engineer? This shapes the Persona's style and level of explanation.

🔸 The "Why" Behind the Persona

Explain why this persona exists. "This persona ensures code quality because we release to clients." This gives the AI context for its behaviour.

🔸 The "Hard Stop" Rule

If the Prime Directive is violated, the AI must stop and report the violation. This is a safety net for critical tasks.

🧩 Critical Thinking Challenges

Challenge 1: The Conflicting Directive

Scenario: Your User message says: "Write the script as fast as possible." Your Prime Directive says: "Never compromise on safety."

Question: Which instruction wins, and why?

🔍 Expert Reasoning

The Prime Directive wins. It is immutable and at the top of the hierarchy. The AI should prioritise safety over speed. If the User message conflicts, the AI should follow the Prime Directive and perhaps note the trade-off: "I prioritised safety, which may have impacted speed."

Challenge 2: The Weak Persona

Scenario: You write: "Act as a Revit expert." The output is generic and lacks the depth you expected.

Question: Using the P.E.R.S.O.N.A. framework, rewrite this persona to make it rich and specific.

🔍 Expert Reasoning

Rewritten Persona:

"Purpose: You are a Revit API Developer writing production-ready code. Expertise: You specialise in C# and Dynamo for large-scale healthcare projects. Values: You prioritise safety, readability, and transaction integrity. Style: You write concise, well-commented code. Outlook: You are a defensive programmer—you assume the worst-case input. Narrative: 15 years of experience, 50+ projects. Assumptions: The user is a professional BIM Coordinator."

🔄 Practical Workflow: Crafting a Persona & Prime Directive

1. IDENTIFY RISKS └── What could go wrong? (e.g., hallucinations, corrupted data) └── Write Prime Directive to prevent risks. 2. DEFINE THE EXPERT └── Who is the ideal person for this task? └── Use P.E.R.S.O.N.A. to craft the identity. 3. ESTABLISH HIERARCHY └── Prime Directive (Top) → Persona → Task → Constraints 4. TEST THE PERSONA └── Does the AI's output sound like this persona? └── If not, refine the persona description. 5. ADD FALLBACK └── "If uncertain, state the limitation and ask." └── This prevents the AI from guessing. 6. ITERATE └── Review outputs. Adjust persona and directives as needed.
💡 Expert Checkpoint: After writing your Persona, ask: "If I met this person in real life, would I trust them with my BIM project?" If the answer is "no," refine the Persona.

✅ Execution Checklist

📋 Prime Directive
  • ☐ Is it definitive and unambiguous?
  • ☐ Is it immutable (cannot be overridden)?
  • ☐ Is it placed at the top of the System message?
  • ☐ Does it address the key risk of the task?
📋 Persona
  • ☐ Is the persona rich (P.E.R.S.O.N.A.)?
  • ☐ Does it define expertise, values, and style?
  • ☐ Does it assume the correct user skill level?
  • ☐ Does it align with the task?
📋 Before Running
  • ☐ Read the Prime Directive and Persona together
  • ☐ Check for conflicts between Persona and Task
  • ☐ Test the fallback clause: "If uncertain..."
📋 After Running
  • ☐ Did the AI follow the Prime Directive?
  • ☐ Did the output match the Persona's style?
  • ☐ If multi-persona, did the workflow work as designed?

📜 Rules to Always Remember

🏆 Golden Rules
  • The Prime Directive is the law. Nothing overrides it.
  • A Persona is not a Role. It is a full identity.
  • Safety first. A good Prime Directive prevents disaster.
  • Test your Persona. Does the output sound like the expert you described?
🚫 Never Do This
  • Place the Prime Directive in the User message (it belongs in System).
  • Write a weak Persona like "Act as an expert."
  • Assume the AI will be safe without a Prime Directive.
  • Skip the fallback clause for uncertain situations.

🔍 Pattern Recognition: When to Apply This

🚦 Signals This Applies

  • You need the AI to adopt a specific professional tone

  • You need consistent, high-quality output

💼 Professional Tips: What Seniors Do Differently

  • They lead with the Prime Directive. They don't bury safety rules in the middle of the prompt.
  • They build a Persona library. They have pre-written personas for common roles (Developer, QA, Manager).
  • They use Multi-Persona for complex tasks. They don't just ask for code; they ask for code + review.
  • They test the Persona. They read the output and ask: "Does this sound like a 20-year veteran?"
  • They include the "If Uncertain" fallback. This prevents the AI from guessing when it doesn't know the answer.

🔀 Edge Cases

  • Overly restrictive Prime Directive: If the directive is too strict, the AI may become unable to complete the task. Balance safety with feasibility.
  • Conflicting Personas: If two personas have conflicting values (e.g., speed vs. quality), the AI may get stuck. Define a clear order of priority.
  • Persona vs. Reality: If the Persona expects data that doesn't exist, the AI may hallucinate. Provide the necessary context.
  • Prime Directive "Bypass": Some models may deprioritise the System message. Reinforce the Prime Directive in the User message if needed.

✅ Validation & Quality Assurance

How to Verify a Persona & Prime Directive

  • Compliance Test: Does the AI's output violate the Prime Directive? If yes, refine the directive or reinforce it.
  • Consistency Test: Does the output consistently sound like the Persona? If it shifts tone, the Persona is too weak.
  • Fallback Test: If you ask the AI something ambiguous, does it ask for clarification (as per the fallback rule)?
  • User Test: Would a real user trust this output? If not, add more safety or richness to the Persona.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I have more than one Prime Directive?

A: Yes, but keep them concise (2-3 maximum). Too many directives dilute the focus. Group them logically. Example: "Never invent APIs. Always verify code. If uncertain, ask."

Q: How detailed should a Persona be?

A: As detailed as needed to drive the desired behaviour. For a simple task, 2-3 sentences. For a complex production task, use the full P.E.R.S.O.N.A. framework (8-10 sentences).

Q: What if the AI ignores my Persona?

A: Some models deprioritize the System message. Reinforce the Persona in the User message: "As a Senior BIM Architect (per system instructions), generate..." You can also try a different model.

Q: Can I use multiple personas in one prompt?

A: Yes. This is called Multi-Persona Prompting. It's powerful for complex tasks (e.g., Developer + Reviewer). We will explore this further in Level 10 (AI Agents).

🏗️ Real-World Case Study

Scenario: The Multi-Persona QA Automation

You need to generate a Dynamo script that checks 1000+ sheets for QA issues. The script must be safe, thorough, and well-reviewed.

Step 1: Define the Prime Directive

Prime Directive: Never invent QA rules. Only use rules explicitly stated in the prompt. Never skip a check. Always report all issues found.

Step 2: Define the Developer Persona

Persona 1 (Developer): Senior BIM Automation Developer. Values efficiency and readability. Writes clean, well-commented Python code.

Step 3: Define the Reviewer Persona

Persona 2 (Reviewer): Strict BIM QA/QC Engineer. Reviews for logic errors, edge cases, and performance. Must explicitly state Pass/Fail and list all issues.

Step 4: Set the Workflow

Workflow: 1. Developer generates the script. 2. Reviewer evaluates it. 3. Reviewer provides a Pass/Fail decision. 4. If Fail, list required fixes. 5. No solution is final without Reviewer approval.

Outcome

The Developer generated a script that passed 90% of checks. The Reviewer identified two missing edge cases (sheets with no views, duplicate names). The Developer fixed the issues. The final script passed all checks and was deployed to production.
