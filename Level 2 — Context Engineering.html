📡 Level 2 — Context Engineering

Permanent Knowledge Base • BIM Automation Specialist • Grounding AI Responses in Reality

📌 Executive Summary

Context Engineering is the discipline of selecting, structuring, and prioritising the background information you feed to an LLM. It is not just about adding more text—it is about adding the right text in the right order, with the right emphasis.

The Funnel Model structures context from Broad (project, industry) to Specific (standards, version) to Immediate (task, constraints). This mirrors how a professional briefs a colleague.

Key Insight: A prompt without context is like giving a surgeon a scalpel and saying "operate" without telling them the patient, the organ, or the condition. Context is what turns an AI from a generic guesser into a domain-specific expert.

🧩 Key Concepts

1. Context Window & Token Limits

Definition: The maximum number of tokens an LLM can process in a single input. Modern models range from 4K (older) to 1M+ tokens (Gemini 1.5, Claude 3).

Implication: You must prioritise. You cannot feed the AI everything. Use the funnel model to select the most critical information.

2. The Funnel Model

  • Broad Context (The "World"): Industry, project type, stakeholders, high-level goals.
  • Specific Context (The "Domain"): Software versions, standards, naming conventions, company policies.
  • Immediate Context (The "Task"): The exact input data, performance constraints, error handling requirements, output format.
💡 Why it works: The AI processes broad context to understand the environment, then specific context to understand the rules, then immediate context to execute the task.

3. Explicit vs. Implicit Context

  • Explicit: "Revit 2025, TransactionMode.Manual." — Stated directly.
  • Implicit: Mentioning "Revit External Command" infers `Execute()` and `UIApplication`.
⚠️ Danger of Implicit Context: The AI may infer incorrectly. Always state version numbers, standards, and constraints explicitly.

4. Grounding with Documentation

Injecting relevant API documentation, company standards, or project-specific information into the prompt. This reduces hallucination and ensures the AI uses the correct methods.

5. Context Verification

Asking the AI to summarise the context before generating the final output. This tests whether the AI understood the constraints correctly.

🧠 Expert Mental Model

🧩 Mental Model: The Briefing Room

Imagine you are a general briefing a lieutenant. You don't just say, "Go capture the hill." You give a full briefing:

  • Situation: Enemy position, terrain, our troops.
  • Mission: Capture the hill by dawn.
  • Constraints: Use light infantry; do not cross the river.
  • Resources: Two platoons, one helicopter, a map.
  • Rules of Engagement: Do not fire unless fired upon.
  • Contingencies: If heavy resistance, retreat and call artillery.

That is Context Engineering. You are briefing the AI with everything it needs to succeed, including constraints, resources, and fallback plans.

How an Expert Thinks About Context

  • Prioritisation: "What is the 20% of context that will drive 80% of the outcome?"
  • Verification: "I will ask the AI to confirm my constraints before it starts."
  • Injection: "I will paste the relevant API docs into the prompt."
  • Iteration: "If the AI asks a question, that question becomes context for the next turn."

🔮 Hidden Knowledge (Rarely Written in Tutorials)

🎯 The "Context Sandwich" Technique

Place the most important context first and last in your prompt. AI models pay more attention to the beginning and end of the context window.

Example: "Revit 2025" at the start and again at the end.

🎯 The "Question Loop" for Context

Instead of providing all context upfront, use this sequence:

  1. Ask the AI what it needs to know
  2. Provide that specific context
  3. Ask the AI to generate the solution

This ensures the AI receives exactly what it needs, not what you think it needs.

🎯 The "Version Context" Trap

Never say "latest version." Say "Revit 2025" or "Dynamo 2.19." The AI's training data cut-off may not include the latest version. Be explicit.

🎯 "Context as Documentation"

In production, your context block becomes the requirements document. A well-written context section can be extracted and used as project documentation.

📌 Things People Usually Skip (But Experts Don't)

🔸 The "Why" Context

Explain why you need the script. "This is for a client deliverable due Friday" helps the AI prioritise robustness over elegance.

🔸 The "Who" Context

Who will run this? A junior coordinator? A senior engineer? This influences the level of comments, error messages, and UI complexity.

🔸 The "Failure" Context

What happens if the script fails? "If this script fails, we have to manually rename 500 sheets." This adds urgency and drives the AI to add safety measures.

🔸 The "Environment" Context

What else is running? "There are 50 other scripts in this project. Avoid naming conflicts." This prevents collisions.

🧩 Critical Thinking Challenges

Challenge 1: The Hidden Assumption

Scenario: You write: "Generate a script to export sheets to Excel."

Question: What five implicit assumptions is the AI likely making, and how would you make them explicit?

🔍 Expert Reasoning

Implicit Assumptions:

  1. Revit is the source. (Explicit: "Export from Revit 2025.")
  2. Excel is the target. (Explicit: "Export to .xlsx file.")
  3. All sheet parameters are exported. (Explicit: "Export Name, Number, and Discipline only.")
  4. No transaction is needed. (Explicit: "This is a read-only operation.")
  5. Small number of sheets. (Explicit: "Handle 500+ sheets efficiently.")

Making these explicit removes all ambiguity.

Challenge 2: The Outdated API

Scenario: You ask the AI to generate Revit API code for Revit 2025. The AI uses a method that was deprecated in Revit 2023.

Question: How would you prevent this using context engineering?

🔍 Expert Reasoning

Prevention:

  1. State the version explicitly: "Revit 2025 API."
  2. Inject the relevant documentation snippet for the method you want.
  3. Add a constraint: "Use only methods available in Revit 2025. If a method is deprecated, use the recommended alternative."
  4. Ask the AI to verify: "Before generating code, confirm you are using Revit 2025 APIs."

🔄 Practical Workflow: Context Engineering

1. GATHER CONTEXT └── Collect: project type, versions, standards, constraints 2. STRUCTURE WITH FUNNEL ├── Broad: Project, industry, stakeholders ├── Specific: Versions, standards, naming conventions └── Immediate: Task, performance, error handling, output 3. INJECT DOCUMENTATION └── Paste relevant API docs, standards snippets 4. VERIFY UNDERSTANDING └── Ask AI: "Summarise the key constraints before you start." 5. GENERATE & ITERATE └── If AI asks clarifying questions, add answers as context 6. REVIEW & IMPROVE └── Did the AI use the context correctly? If not, refine.
💡 Expert Checkpoint: After Step 4, if the AI's summary doesn't match your intent, you know the context is ambiguous. Refine before generating the final output.

📜 Rules to Always Remember

🏆 Golden Rules
  • Relevance over volume. Short, relevant context beats long, generic context.
  • Never rely on implicit. State everything explicitly.
  • Ground with docs. Inject documentation to prevent hallucinations.
  • Verify understanding. Ask the AI to summarise context before execution.
🚫 Never Do This
  • Say "latest version" — always specify version numbers
  • Assume the AI knows your company standards
  • Provide context in a random, unstructured order
  • Skip the verification step

🔍 Pattern Recognition: When to Apply This

🚦 Signals This Applies
  • You are generating code for a specific software version
  • You are working with company-specific standards
  • You need the AI to avoid hallucinations
  • You want production-ready, not generic, output
⚠️ Warning Signs You're Forgetting This
  • You haven't specified the Revit or Dynamo version
  • You assume the AI knows your naming convention
  • You haven't provided any error handling constraints

💼 Professional Tips: What Seniors Do Differently

  • They use context templates. "Here is our standard context block for all Revit tasks: version, standards, QA/QC requirements."
  • They inject documentation directly. They don't just mention the API—they paste the relevant documentation snippet into the prompt.
  • They verify context. They ask the AI to summarise constraints before generating code.
  • They treat context as version-controlled. They update their context blocks as standards and versions change.
  • They think about the "user" context. They specify who will run the script and adjust the complexity accordingly.

⚡ Optimization Opportunities

🚀 Performance
  • Use structured formatting (Markdown, JSON) for context to improve AI parsing
  • Use bullet points, not dense paragraphs
  • Prioritise: put the most critical context first
📊 Quality
  • Add "context verification" as a step in the prompt
  • Include examples (few-shot) to show how context should be applied
  • Use negative constraints: "Do not use X; use Y instead."
🤖 Automation
  • Create reusable context templates for common tasks
  • Automate context gathering (e.g., read version from file)
  • Use RAG to retrieve relevant context from a knowledge base

🔀 Edge Cases

  • Context overload: Too much context can dilute the AI's focus. Use the funnel model to prioritise.
  • Outdated context: If you copy a prompt from an older project, the context may be wrong. Always review and update.
  • Conflicting context: If you provide conflicting information (e.g., "Revit 2024" and "Revit 2025 API"), the AI may get confused. Be consistent.
  • Missing critical context: Sometimes you don't know what you don't know. Ask the AI: "What additional context would help you generate the best solution?"

✅ Validation & Quality Assurance

How to Verify Context Engineering

  • Verification prompt: "Before you begin, summarise the key constraints in your own words."
  • Check against requirements: Does the AI's summary match your intent?
  • Test with a minimal example: Run the generated code on a small test case to verify it honours the context.
  • Review for hallucinations: Does the AI mention any APIs or methods that don't exist in your specified version?

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How much context is too much?

A: When the AI starts ignoring your core instruction because it's distracted by the context, you've added too much. A good rule of thumb: if your context section is longer than your task section, review it. Use the funnel model to prioritise.

Q: Should I always ask the AI to summarise context?

A: For production-level tasks, yes. It's a cheap check that ensures the AI understood your constraints. If the summary is wrong, you can correct it before the AI spends tokens generating unusable code.

Q: What if the AI ignores my context?

A: Some models deprioritise context. If this happens, reinforce the context in the User message: "As stated in the context, we use Revit 2025. Do not use deprecated methods." You can also try a different model (Claude is often better at following context than GPT-4).

Q: Can I use RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) for context?

A: Yes. RAG is a form of context engineering where you retrieve relevant documents from a knowledge base and inject them into the prompt. We will cover RAG in detail in Level 8.

📝 Prompt Improvement Suggestions

Your Level 1 Expert Prompt (Without Rich Context)

"Act as a Senior BIM Automation Specialist. Generate a Dynamo workflow that renames sheets using 'OriginalName N'. Provide: node list, Python code, error handling, performance optimisation."

Weaknesses: Lacks project context, version specifics, detailed constraints, and validation steps.

Production-Level with Context Engineering

System: You are a Senior BIM Automation Architect. Our firm specialises in healthcare projects. We use Revit 2024 and Dynamo 2.13. Task: Generate a Dynamo script that renames sheets by appending a sequential number. Context: - Project: 500+ sheets, 20 levels, MEP + Architectural + Structural disciplines. - Naming standard: 'A-{Level}-{Discipline}-{Number}'. - Existing names: "A-01-ARCH-001". - New names: "A-01-ARCH-001 1", "A-01-ARCH-001 2". - Backup parameter: '_BKP_OriginalName' must be populated. - Performance: 500 sheets in under 15 seconds. - Error Handling: Duplicate names → append '_X'. - End-user: Junior BIM coordinator—add comments and clear error messages. Output: Python code, node diagram, CSV log format.

Why it's better: Every piece of context is explicit, structured, and grounded. The AI has no room to guess.

🎯 Final Expert Takeaway

📄 One Page of Advice from a 30-Year Mentor

"Context is not a luxury—it is a necessity."

Every time you skip context, you are asking the AI to guess. And the AI will guess wrong—not because it is bad, but because it has no choice. Without context, the AI is a generalist. With context, it becomes a specialist.

The secret: The best prompts are 70% context and 30% instruction. The instruction is the "what"; the context is the "why," "where," "when," "who," and "how."

🔥 Ground the AI in your reality. Give it the briefing it deserves.

🏗️ Real-World Case Study

Scenario: The 1000-Sheet Hospital Project

You are the BIM Manager on a large hospital project. The project has 1000+ sheets. The client has just requested a new naming convention. You need to rename all sheets quickly and reliably.

Step 1: Gather Context

  • Version: Revit 2025, Dynamo 2.15
  • Project: Hospital, 15 levels, MEP + Architectural + Structural
  • Naming Standard: "H-{Level}-{Discipline}-{Number}" (e.g., H-01-A-001)
  • Existing Names: "H-01-A-001" (no suffix)
  • New Names: "H-01-A-001 1", "H-01-A-001 2"
  • Backup Parameter: '_BKP_OriginalName' must exist
  • Performance: 1000 sheets in under 30 seconds
  • End-user: Junior BIM coordinator

Step 2: Write the Prompt with Context

System: You are a Senior BIM Automation Architect. Our firm specialises in healthcare. We use Revit 2025 and Dynamo 2.15. Task: Generate a Dynamo script that renames sheets using 'OriginalName N'. Context: - Project: Hospital, 15 levels, 1000+ sheets. - Naming Standard: "H-{Level}-{Discipline}-{Number}". - Existing: "H-01-A-001". - New: "H-01-A-001 1", "H-01-A-001 2". - Backup: '_BKP_OriginalName' must be populated. - Performance: 1000 sheets in under 30 seconds. - Duplicate Handling: Append '_X' if duplicate exists. - End-user: Junior coordinator—add comments and clear errors. Output: Python code, node diagram, CSV log format.

Step 3: Verify Context

Add to the prompt: "Before generating the script, summarise the key constraints in your own words."

Outcome

The AI generated a script that processed 1000 sheets in 12 seconds. The junior coordinator ran it successfully. The CSV log provided an audit trail for the client.
